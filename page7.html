Who Can Have Your Blood?

Which blood types can be given to different people

Introducing blood donation to a younger audience through an exhibition will get them to associate it with something fun. The purposes it to get kids and young adult used to the idea, so when they are older, they are more likely to donate

Three Stage Plan

1

First Glance

This will be placed in a children's museum, the bright colours will attract children to want to interact with it

2

Interaction

When a child places their hand on one of the letters on the left, the corresponding blood types will light up

3

Understanding

The child will gain some insight on which blood types can be donated to or associate that blood type with many different blood recipients

The code used for the exhibition! I used Micobit coders to create the interactive part of the exhibition. Javascript was used to write the same code, that was uploaded onto each of the coders. This would allow the LED lights to turn off and on, depending on whether a person touched the letter that had conductive paint behind them. 

First design

This design followed the grid theme where each blood droplet and each letter would have its own quadrant. so viewers would be able to decipher which blood droplet belonged where

Second design

The darkness of the contrasting colours were reduced to show the colours around where each of the lights would be. This is to make sure that the light contrasts as much as possible with the background

Third design

This design was the final template for the interior, as their would be more space for the LEDs to shine light out of. This would provide the desired effect better than the previous design, that has less light space

The design was based on one of the top five fallacies that a person's blood type is useless, In fact every blood type if useful, because each compound can be used

Blood donation is split into three major categories;. These consist of red blood cells, white blood cells and platelets. Each one of these can be donated individually or as a whole, depending on the circumstances. More often than not, platelet donation is just as important as blood donation. However, unlike blood donation the demand for different components can change very quickly 

This design showcases how an interactive exhibition will be wired together. This will incorporate several Micocoders, that will have a more conducive shape painted behind each of the letters on the right. When a hand is placed over the letter, this will cause the corresponding led to turn on. To avoid any short circuits, the LEDs will be attached with wires and the touch sensors will be attached with conductive strips.
