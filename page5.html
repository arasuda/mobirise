Pocket Provication

A keyring that the public can take away, so more people will register to donate 

Most events provide a take-away item(s) so people can remember the message of the event. If a person is given a keyring from a blood donation event, this may include spake conversations and spread awareness. 

Three Stage Plan

1

Dispertion

These will be handed out after blood donations, colleges and university with flyers on blood donation

2

Interatction

Once a phone touches the keyring, blood.com will be open so people can learn more and register.

3

Donation

Once signed up more people will know where their nearest blood donation centre is and will donate

These designs are all based on a basic key ring theme. The reason I chose this for my provocation is that many people collect keyrings from different people, events and places even if they don't support the meaning behind the keyring. This makes these designs more suitable for my advance as it not only covers people, a wide range of people of different age groups; as well as backgrounds

Layour of the blood bag

the chip

The bottom circle is not part of the model. Its is a component to show where the chip would be located. The chip is 25mm in diameter.

Front Design

Each of the designs would be identical except for the label at the front. Each of these labels would be a blood type

Sides & Edges

Each of the sides has smooth edges, to prevent injury and to mimic the layout of the blood bag. Only the text and the label have sharp edges.

Take Away Key Ring 

This would be a new feature NHS blood hand owt to spread awareness

Size

It is approximately 35 x 65 x 10 mm. The microchip located on the flat back is 25mm,

Interactivity

The keyring contains a chip, so when a phone is placed over it, the phone will open a link to Blood.com online registration.

Loctaion

These would be given to donors on their second donation, as the NHS would know their blood type.

Personalise

There will be 8 different key rings for the different blood groups. The will be given to the people that correspond to through blood groups.
